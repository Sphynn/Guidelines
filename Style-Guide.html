Color

Colors are one of the most important representations of a brand as they make an instant impact on the viewer. Specific colors have been selected to represent identities and to help establish immediate brand recognition. In order to maintain the integrity of the brand, these colors must be used consistently across printing processes and digital display.

  • CMYK: This 4-color system is used for full color printing, also known as Process printing.
  • RGB: The (Red, Green, Blue) color system is meant specifically for screen display. (Ex: PowerPoint)
  • LAB: Lab color space is used when graphics for print have to be converted from RGB to CMYK.
  • HSB: The hue, saturation and brightness system is the representations of points in an RGB color model used by color pickers.
  • HEX: The web color system is meant specifically for online use.

Logo

Logos are the touchstone of the brand and one of our most valuable assets. We must ensure proper usage.

Minimum Size
The minimum size of the logo must be no smaller than 100 px wide or 2.65 cm on printed materials.

Clear Zone
The minimum required clear space around the logo must be equal to the x-height of the lower-case letters in the logo.

Incorrect Logo Use

  1. Do not redesign or redraw the logo by any means.
  2. Do not use any color other than the approved colors for the logo.
  3. Do not replace the approved typeface.
  4. Do not distort or space out logo elements.
  5. Do not add an animation.
  6. Do not alter the proportions.
  7. Do not use the container shape as a stroke/hollowed container.
  8. Do not change the opacity.
  9. Do not add a dropshadow.
  10. Do not add a stroke.
  11. Do not bevel or emboss.
  12. Do not extrude.
  13. Do not add any other new elements.
  14. Do not add type elements in violation of clear space rules.
  15. Do not frame in a shape.
  16. Do not place on an angle.
  17. Do not add a reflection.
